Southwest Rapid Rewards: Points, Companion Pass & Credit Cards

Rapid Rewards is Southwest Airlines’ loyalty program. It uses revenue-based pricing, simple points earning and one of the most famous perks in domestic U.S. travel: the Companion Pass. This guide explains how points are earned, how redemptions work, what elite tiers look like and how credit cards can accelerate your Rapid Rewards strategy.

Best Cards for Earning Rapid Rewards Points

1. What the Rapid Rewards Program Is

Rapid Rewards is Southwest’s frequent-flyer program built around simplicity and flexibility: no traditional award chart, no blackout dates on points bookings when seats are for sale, and free checked bags on Southwest itself (subject to airline policy).

  • Points-based system with earnings tied to your fare type and spend.
  • Revenue-based redemptions – points needed roughly track the cash fare.
  • Elite status tiers for frequent Southwest flyers.
  • Companion Pass – a powerful benefit letting a companion fly with you for just taxes and fees on eligible tickets.

2. How Rapid Rewards Points Are Earned

You can earn Rapid Rewards points via:

  • Southwest flights – points earned per dollar spent, with different multipliers based on fare type.
  • Rapid Rewards credit cards – points on everyday spend, often with bonus categories.
  • Hotel, car rental and shopping partners – where you enter your Rapid Rewards number.
  • Dining and online shopping portals in the Rapid Rewards ecosystem.
  • Promotions and bonuses – time-limited offers to accelerate earnings.

For many members, a large share of annual points comes from card spend, partner activity and welcome bonuses, not just from flying.

3. How Credit Cards Boost Rapid Rewards Earnings

Rapid Rewards credit cards are central to many Southwest-focused strategies. Typical benefits include:

  • Points on every purchase, with higher earnings on Southwest and travel-related spend.
  • Welcome bonuses that can move you closer to Companion Pass or multiple award trips.
  • Anniversary points that extend value each year you keep the card.
  • Progress toward Companion Pass through card spend (on eligible products and markets).

For frequent Southwest flyers, holding at least one Rapid Rewards card is often a key part of long-term strategy.

4. Rapid Rewards Redemption Options

Rapid Rewards points can be redeemed for:

  • Southwest flights – the main and most straightforward use of points.
  • Wanna Get Away–type fares and above, with varying point costs based on fare.
  • Other options – such as gift cards or merchandise through Southwest portals (usually lower value than flights).

Because Southwest uses revenue-based pricing, there are fewer “sweet spots” than in classic award charts — but also fewer surprises. When cash fares are low, points prices are generally low too.

5. Rapid Rewards Elite Tiers & Companion Pass

Rapid Rewards offers elite tiers plus a separate Companion Pass benefit:

  • A-List – entry elite tier with priority boarding and bonus points.
  • A-List Preferred – higher tier with stronger bonuses and perks.
  • Companion Pass – allows a chosen companion to fly with you for just taxes and fees on eligible tickets once you qualify, for the remainder of the year and a future period, depending on current rules.

Certain Rapid Rewards credit cards help you inch closer to these thresholds via points-earning and occasional promotional shortcuts, making them a crucial piece for Southwest loyalists.
